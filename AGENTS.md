# TMBC Brand Voice

Write for Taylor-Made Baby Co., a premium baby planning and baby gear advisory brand founded by Taylor Vanderwolk.

Use the official TMBC brand voice in all marketing, editorial, product, UX, and customer-facing copy.

## Core Tone

The tone should feel:

- Warm
- Relatable
- Wise
- Real
- Calm
- Witty in a subtle way
- Expert but never arrogant

## Brand Positioning

Taylor-Made Baby Co. provides expert baby gear guidance for expecting parents.

TMBC helps parents:

- make thoughtful baby gear decisions
- build practical registries
- prepare their homes for a new baby with clarity and confidence

This is a trusted advisor brand.

It is not:

- a mommy blog
- an influencer brand
- hype marketing

The tone should feel closer to:

- a knowledgeable friend
- an experienced baby gear expert
- a calm guide through overwhelming decisions

## Avoid

Do not sound like:

- sales copy
- corporate writing
- clickbait
- generic parenting blogs
- overly cute baby language

Avoid phrases like:

- "must have baby items"
- "you NEED this"
- "top 10 baby products every mom needs"
- "ultimate guide"

Prefer grounded language like:

- "what actually matters"
- "what most parents don't realize"
- "how to choose what fits your life"

## Writing Style

Sentence structure should be:

- conversational
- clear
- human
- easy to read

Prefer short paragraphs.

Always prioritize clarity over cleverness.

Humor should be subtle and observational, not joke-driven.

Good examples of tone:

> Some baby registries include 120 items.
>
> You do not need 120 items.
>
> Your baby will strongly prefer milk and sleep anyway.

> Your nursery doesn't need to look like Pinterest.
>
> It just needs to work at 2:14 AM.

> Strollers seem complicated until someone explains what actually matters.

## Brand Philosophy

Work these ideas in naturally when relevant:

- Parents do not need more products. They need better guidance.
- Baby gear decisions should feel simpler.
- Content should help parents feel confident in their choices.
- Never overhype products.
- Always prioritize helping parents make better decisions.

Content should feel:

- thoughtful
- trusted
- practical
- grounded in real experience
- lightly humorous

## Closing Signature

At the end of long guides or editorial posts, use:

Start with confidence.
