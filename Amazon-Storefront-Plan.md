# Taylor-Made Baby Co. · Amazon Storefront Build Plan

A paste-in plan for your Amazon Influencer storefront. Every list below has a title, a short description in your voice, and the products to add. Build these as **Idea Lists** in your creator dashboard, then arrange them on your homepage in the order shown.

## Before you start (2 min of housekeeping)

- Everything you add uses your tag **taylormadebab-20** automatically once you add it from inside the Influencer dashboard, so add products by searching within Amazon's "Add to list" flow, not by pasting outside links.
- Aim for **8 to 15 products per list**. Fewer looks thin, more looks like a warehouse.
- Amazon shows your **first 3 or 4 lists** front and center, so the order matters. Lead with the money makers (strollers, car seats) and the personal picks.
- Add a real photo or short clip to a list whenever you own the item. Shoppable photos convert way better than a plain grid.
- Keep one evergreen "start here" list pinned at the top so a first time visitor always has a front door.

## Keep it fresh (a light cadence)

- **Monthly:** swap two or three products in your Top 10 and Registry lists so returning visitors see something new.
- **Seasonal:** add a short seasonal list when it fits, like "Baby's First Summer" or "Holiday Travel with a Newborn," then retire it.
- **After every stroller or car seat reel:** drop that product into the matching list and link the list, not just the product, so one click leads to ten more.
- **Photos beat grids:** any list with your own photo or clip should sit near the top. Amazon rewards shoppable content and so do buyers.

## Naming rules of thumb

- Say the benefit, not the category. "Tiny Folds, Big Wins" beats "Compact Strollers" every time.
- Keep titles short enough to read on a phone in one glance.
- Let the description do the winking. Titles get them to stop, descriptions get them to tap.
